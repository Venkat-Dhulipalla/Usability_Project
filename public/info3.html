What Role Should Students Play in Combating Ragging?

Students have a crucial role to play in combating ragging:

a. Zero Tolerance: Students should adopt a zero-tolerance attitude towards ragging. They must understand that it is not a harmless tradition but a serious offense.

b. Support Victims: If a student witnesses ragging or knows of a victim, they should provide support and report the incident to the appropriate authorities. Supporting victims emotionally is essential.

c. Active Bystander Intervention: Students should intervene to stop any ragging incident they encounter, safely if possible, or by seeking help from authorities.

d. Participate in Awareness: Students can actively participate in awareness campaigns and programs aimed at preventing ragging. Spreading the word about the consequences of ragging is vital.

e. Mentoring and Peer Support: Senior students should focus on mentoring and providing peer support to juniors to create a positive and inclusive environment.

f. Report Anonymously: In cases where fear of retaliation exists, students can utilize anonymous reporting mechanisms to report ragging incidents.

g. Resist Peer Pressure: Students should resist peer pressure to engage in ragging activities, even if they feel pressured to do so by their peers.
